What is Censorship?

According to the Oxford dictionary, censorship is "the suppression or prohibition of any parts of books, films, news, etc. that are considered obscene, politically unacceptable, or a threat to security." This raises the question though, who decides what is obscene, what is politically unacceptable, and what counts as a threat to security?

The American Civil Liberties Union provides a better definition in their article "What is Censorship," they say that censorship is "the suppression of words, images, or ideas that are 'offensive,' happens whenever some people succeed in imposing their personal political or moral values on others." This definition succeeds where the other fails as it highlights the fact that censorship is a tool of those in power to silence the opposition.

What does this mean?

With the world the way that it is, censorship has been used throughout history and continues to be used to stamp out marginalized voices. Today, censorship impacts queer BIPOC communities through many methods such as online content moderation and book banning.

Online Censorship

The internet is a big place, there are many ways that censorship has been used online to harm marginalized communities, but most of it comes down to content moderation. Learn more about internet censorship and how people have fought back on the online censorship page.

Book Banning

Historically, the banning of books as been a huge aspect of censorship, and that has not changed today. Many books depicting queer characters and characters of color have been banned from schools and libraries. More on book banning and what's being done to stop it on the book banning page.
